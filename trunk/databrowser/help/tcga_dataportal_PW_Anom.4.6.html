Viewing a Pathway Map onto the Cell Morphology
The top of the page is the selected biochemical pathway projected onto cell morphology.
If the SVG plug-in is not installed on your computer, the cell diagram does not displayed. For more information about installing the SVG plug-in, see Installing the SVG Plug-in on page 6.
The map is essentially a Biocarta pathway diagram. Three main cellular components (nucleus, cytoplasm and cellular membrane) are shown along with graphical elements that represent individual proteins. To display information about a gene, follow these steps:
1.
To access this page, select a pathway on the Viewing Pathways and Associate Abnormalities page. The Biocarta pathway diagram appears at the top of the page.
Caution:
Do not use the Back button in your browser to return to the pathway list. Scroll to the top of the page and click Return to Pathway List.
2.
To display gene information, click on a gene name on the diagram.
The UCSC Genome Browser opens showing the selected gene. For complete documentation, click the Help button in the Browser.
To review anomaly information about the genes associated with the pathway, see Reviewing Genes with Potential Abnormalities.
