Interpreting the Results
Some of the results may be indicative of tumor subclasses in the set, like this tumor antigen:
4110    "melanoma antigen family A, 11" 1       2       39      42
4112    "melanoma antigen family B, 1"  10      2       30      42
In the example, one version of a gene might be overexpressed in 10 samples, and the other version shows no such preference. This result may be reproduced with more rigorous statistical tests.
The data may be loaded into a database as a test, even if it contains a fair amount of noise. This type of computation was suggested by NCI scientists as a quick way to generate sample output for a portal prototype. Formally, we cannot compare an individual sample to the average of a group of 42 samples of which this sample is a member. But we may compare a sample to an average of other 41 samples in a group. Average of 41 samples is not much different from an average of 42 samples.
